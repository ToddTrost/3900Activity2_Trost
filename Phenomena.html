Solar Phenomena


Geomagnetic Storms


Geomagnetic storms are caused by interactions from the Sun's solar wind via a high speed stream (HSS) or coronal mass ejection (CME) bombarding us with electrons.

This phenomenon usually leads to high frequency (HF) communication impacts in the polar regions, and is the cause of the aurora.

Under the right conditions, and extended periods of storming, resultant charged particle build-up may lead to satellite drag issues or internal component damage.

Solar Flares


Solar flares are eruptions from localized areas on the surface of the sun called sunspots that produce intense level of electromagnetic radiation.

This phenomenon leads to an array of impacts from X-Ray radiation, proton particle related events, HF communication blackouts, and triggering CMEs.

Coronal Mass Ejections (CME)


Coronal Mass Ejections (or CMEs for short) are large expulsions of plasma and magnetic fields from the Sun's corona.

Generally, they lead to geomagnetic storming, usually of a greater intensity than storming caused by Coronal Hole high speed streams
